"The Psychology of Decision-Making" explores the intricate mechanisms underlying human choice processes, encompassing cognitive, emotional, and social dimensions. This course delves into the theories and research methods used to unravel the complexities of decision-making, examining how individuals assess options, weigh risks and benefits, and ultimately arrive at choices. From classical models to contemporary perspectives, students will investigate biases, heuristics, and cultural influences shaping decision outcomes across various domains such as economics, healthcare, and everyday life. Through case studies and interactive exercises, participants will develop a nuanced understanding of decision-making processes, equipping them with practical tools to navigate complex choices more effectively in personal and professional contexts.
